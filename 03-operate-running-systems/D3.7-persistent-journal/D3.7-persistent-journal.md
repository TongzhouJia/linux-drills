# KP D3.7 — Preserve System Journals (persistent journald)
**5 tasks · RHEL 10 · server1 as root**

**1.** Determine whether journald is currently storing logs **persistently** or only in memory, and save your finding to `/root/journal_storage.txt`.

**2.** Configure journald to keep logs **persistently across reboots** (edit the config, create the required directory, and apply the change).

**3.** After enabling persistence and rebooting, verify you can list **multiple boots** with `journalctl --list-boots`.

**4.** Limit the persistent journal to a maximum of **500M** of disk usage.

**5.** Display the journal from the **previous** boot (only possible once persistence is on).
