# KP D4.4 — Mount at Boot by UUID / LABEL
**6 tasks · RHEL 10 · server2 as root**

**1.** Find the **UUID** of `/dev/data_vg/data_lv` and save it to `/root/uuid.txt`.

**2.** Add an `/etc/fstab` entry that mounts that filesystem at `/mnt/data_lv` persistently **by UUID**, then verify with `mount -a` (must not error).

**3.** Give an **xfs** filesystem the label `DATA`, then mount it at `/mnt/labeled` in fstab **by LABEL**.

**4.** Give an **ext4** filesystem a label using `e2label`, and mount it by label in fstab.

**5.** Validate the entire `/etc/fstab` for errors **without rebooting** (e.g. `findmnt --verify` and/or `mount -a`).

**6.** Unmount `/mnt/data_lv`, then confirm it mounts again automatically via `mount -a` (simulating a reboot).
